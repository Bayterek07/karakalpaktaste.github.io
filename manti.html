Manti

Manti are traditional Central Asian steamed dumplings made with thin dough and a rich, finely chopped meat filling. They are hearty, savory, and often served as a festive or everyday dish.

prep time

55 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

minimum 1

Tags : Uzbek / Karakalpak cuisine Manti maindish traditional

instructions

step 1

Mix flour, water, salt (and egg if used) to make an elastic dough. Let it rest for 20-30 minutes. Prepare the meat filling by finely chopping meat and onions, and mixing with fat, salt, and spices.

step 2

Roll out the dough thinly and cut into squares (8-10cm). Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each square. Fold and seal to form a pouch or envelope shape

step 3

Steam the manti in a steamer for 40-45 minutes until fully cooked. Serve hot, optionally with butter, yogurt, or sour cream.

ingredients

Wheat flour

Water

Salt

Egg (1-2)

500-700 g meat (lamb or beef)

1 large onion, finely chopped

Fat (lamb fat or kurdyuk, optional)

Spices: black pepper, cumin (zira)

Optional: potato or pumpkin for variation

Butter or oil (for serving)

Optional: sour cream or yogurt for serving
