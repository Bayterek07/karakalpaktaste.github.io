Pirashki

Pirashki is a crispy deep-fried turnover filled with seasoned minced meat, famous for its juicy interior and thin crunchy crust.

prep time

25 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

1-2

Tags : Central Asian cuisine Fried pastries Traditional food Street food

instructions

step 1

Mix flour, warm water, a pinch of salt, and a little oil to make a soft, elastic dough. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes. Prepare the filling by mixing ground meat with finely chopped onions, salt, and black pepper.

step 2

Divide the dough into small balls and roll each one into a thin round sheet. Place a spoonful of meat filling on one half, fold the dough over, and seal the edges tightly to form a half-moon shape

step 3

Heat plenty of oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Fry the chebureks for 1-2 minutes on each side until golden and crispy. Remove onto paper towels to drain excess oil and serve hot.

ingredients

Wheat flaour

Warm water

Salt

Vegetable oil (for dough and frying)

500-700 g ground meat (beef or lamb)

1-2 onions, finely chopped

Black pepper, salt (to taste)

Optional: a little cumin (zira)
