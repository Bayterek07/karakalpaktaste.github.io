Kespas

Kespas is a traditional Karakalpak (and Central Asian) noodle dish made from very thin wheat-flour dough, rolled into a tube and finely cut into noodles. In Karakalpak cuisine, these noodles are often dressed with milk and sour milk, creating a delicate, comforting dish.

prep time

35 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

minimum 1

Tags : Karakalpak cuisine Kespas maindish traditional

instructions

step 1

Mix wheat flour, water, and a pinch of salt to make a smooth and elastic dough. Let the dough rest for a little while.

step 2

Roll the dough out very thin into a flat sheet. Then, roll that sheet into a tube shape.

step 3

Cut the rolled tube very finely to create thin noodle strips (kespas). Boil or cook the noodles gently (in milk or water, depending on the recipe) until they are soft. Finally, pour milk and sour milk over the cooked kespas to serve, according to Karakalpak tradition.
